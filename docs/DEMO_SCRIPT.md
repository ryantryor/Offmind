## CapCut / Resolve assembly recipe (~30 min)

1. **Project:** 1920√ó1080, 30 fps, MP4 H.264 output.
2. **Track layout:**
   - Track V1: screen captures + stills (13 PNGs)
   - Track V2: title card (cut 10)
   - Track A1: VO cuts 01‚Äì10 (drop in order, snap end-to-end)
   - Track A2: lo-fi piano bed, volume automated to ‚Äì18 dB when A1 has audio, ‚Äì6 dB in the gaps
3. **Ken Burns on stills:** 1.00 ‚Üí 1.05 slow zoom over the full duration of each still. Keeps PNGs from feeling static.
4. **Cut 03 (stream) ‚Äî MUST be a live screen capture,** not a still. The streaming tokens are the pitch. Record with OBS at 1080p60; drop the MP4 onto V1 and align to VO `cut-03-stream.mp3`.
5. **Cut 04 (hover)** can be a 2 s screen capture of the hover interaction, or a still with a mouse-cursor overlay animated in After Effects. Screen capture is faster.
6. **Cut 07 (search fusion)** also benefits from a live capture ‚Äî the re-ranking is the visual hook. If you must use stills, cross-dissolve between the four PNGs at 0.25 s each.
7. **Cut 08 (smoke)** ‚Äî easiest: screen-record the terminal running `./scripts/smoke.sh`. Alternative: screenshot the final "All checks passed" output and slow-pan over the log.
8. **Cut 10 (title)** ‚Äî if you don't have a designer, open the README in a clean browser tab, screenshot the hero block, overlay the URL and "Built on Actian VectorAI DB" in CapCut text tool.
9. **Captions:** burn in English SRT (auto-generate from the VO, verify). Add a bilingual Chinese track as an optional second SRT ‚Äî do NOT dub a Chinese voice.
10. **Export:** MP4 H.264, 1080p, ‚â§ 80 MB for easy YouTube upload.
