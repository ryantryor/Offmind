Got up at six and went for a run at Olympic Park. No real reason — just wanted to be outside.

Did eight kilometers. Stopped by the lake to drink some water and saw an old man doing tai chi, moving so slowly it looked like he was swimming through the air. A kind of stillness came over me. The city wasn't awake yet. The air tasted sweet.

Work stuff, relationship stuff, future stuff — I left all of it back home, next to the dirty mug on my kitchen counter.

When I got home I made fried eggs with way too much pepper. That was the happiest moment of my day.
