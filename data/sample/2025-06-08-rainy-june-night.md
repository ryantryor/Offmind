Raining tonight. Beijing rain always arrives in a rush and leaves in a rush.

I sat by the window, not doing anything, just listening. A cup of tea had gone cold in my hand.

I thought of being small, thunderstorms in summer, Mom pulling me in against her and saying "don't be scared." She seemed infinite to me then. Now I'm taller than her, and I still miss that feeling of being protected.

How do you grow up? I think it happens in a specific instant — you realize the people who can protect you will also get old. Then you start trying to be the person who protects someone else.

The rain is still falling.
