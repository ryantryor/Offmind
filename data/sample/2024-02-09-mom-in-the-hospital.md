Mom collapsed this morning and got admitted. The doctor says it's low blood sugar combined with her blood pressure — nothing serious, two days of observation and she'll be fine. But my hands haven't stopped shaking since the phone call.

Sitting in the hospital corridor on one of those plastic chairs, I suddenly realized something absurd: I haven't been home for dinner in three months. Three.

She was lying there smiling at me, saying "don't worry, go back to work, I'm fine." I nodded. Then I went to the bathroom and cried for ten minutes.

Dad said, "come home on weekends more often, okay?" I said okay. This time I have to actually do it.
