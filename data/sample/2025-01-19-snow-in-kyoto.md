It snowed in Kyoto.

Standing on the steps of Kiyomizu-dera, Mom and I watched the snow fall onto the old roof tiles. She was wearing the red down jacket I bought her last Christmas — a bright spot against the white.

She said she was fifty-something and had never seen this much snow. She grew up in Sichuan; they barely get snow there.

I didn't say anything. I just held her hand a little tighter. Her hand is small and cold.

I want to take her more places this year.
